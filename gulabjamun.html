Gulab-Jamun

cooking time

preparation time :20 cook time:25

ingredients

  1. -1 cup milk powder

  2. -2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

  3. -1/4 teaspoon baking soda

  4. -2 tablespoons ghee or unsalted butter, melted

  5. -A few tablespoons of milk (as needed to make the dough)

  6. -1.5 cups sugar

  7. -1.5 cups water

  8. -1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

  9. -1 teaspoon rose water

  10. -A few strands of saffron (optional)

Instructions

  • Prepare the sugar syrup by dissolving sugar in water over medium heat. Add cardamom powder, saffron, and rose water. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes until it forms a slightly sticky syrup. Set it aside.

  • In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Add the melted ghee or butter and mix well.

  • Gradually add milk, a little at a time, and knead the mixture into a soft and smooth dough. Be careful not to over-knead.Divide the dough into small, smooth balls without any cracks. Make sure they are compact and smooth to ensure even cooking.

  • Heat the oil or ghee in a deep pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, reduce the flame to low and carefully add the prepared dough balls. Fry them on low heat until they turn golden brown, gently rolling them to ensure even cooking.

  • Once the gulab jamuns are evenly golden, remove them from the oil and drain the excess oil by placing them on a paper towel.Place the fried gulab jamuns in the sugar syrup while it is still warm. Let them soak for at least 1-2 hours before serving.

  • Serve the gulab jamuns warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts if desired.
